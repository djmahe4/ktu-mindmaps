# **Indian Constitution: Expanded Exam Clean Sheet (with Memory Aids)**

---

## **I. Preamble**

* **Key Terms:** "We, the People..." giving ourselves this Constitution.
* **S-S-S D R:**

  * **S**overeign (independent)
  * **S**ocialist (welfare, equitable distribution)
  * **S**ecular (state has no religion, treats all equally)
  * **D**emocratic (gov by the people)
  * **R**epublic (elected head of state)
    *→ Think: "SSS DRives the Nation"*
* **J-L-E-F:**

  * **J**ustice (Social, Economic, Political)
  * **L**iberty (Thought, Expression, Belief, Faith, Worship)
  * **E**quality (Status, Opportunity)
  * **F**raternity (Brotherhood, Dignity, Unity)
    *→ Think: "JELF helps build the Nation"*
* **Significance:** "Identity Card" or "Soul/Key" of the Constitution. Shows philosophy & objectives.
* **Interpretative Value:** Helps understand ambiguous provisions.
* **Amendability:** Yes (Art 368), but "Basic Structure" (e.g., secularism, democracy, federalism) cannot be altered (Kesavananda Bharati case).

---

## **II. Citizenship (Part II: Articles 5–11)**

* **Core Idea:** Defines who belongs to India. Parliament has power to make laws.
* **Acquisition:**
  **B-D-R-N-I** — By **B**irth, **D**escent, **R**egistration, **N**aturalization, **I**ncorporation of territory
  *→ Think: "BeDRINI is now an Indian"*
* **Termination/Loss:**
  **R-T-D** — **R**enunciation (you give up), **T**ermination (you take foreign), **D**eprivation (Govt. takes away for fraud/disloyalty)
  *→ Think: "CiTiZenship ReTiDe (retired/removed)"*

---

## **III. Fundamental Rights (FRs) (Part III: Articles 12–35) – *The Core Guarantees***

* **Art 12 (State):** Defines "State" against whom FRs are enforceable (Govt., Parliament, State Leg., Local Authorities, other bodies like LIC, ONGC).
  *→ Think: "Who can violate my rights?"*
* **Art 13 (Judicial Review):** Laws inconsistent with FRs are VOID. SC & HC can strike them down.
  *→ Think: "FRs are Supreme; bad laws get the boot."*

### **Right to Equality (Art 14–18):**

* **Art 14:** Equality before law & Equal protection of laws
  *→ Think: "All are 1 (one) before Law."*
* **Art 15:** No discrimination based on **RR-CSP** — **R**eligion, **R**ace, **C**aste, **S**ex, **P**lace of birth
  *→ Think: "No RR-CSP bias."*
* **Art 16:** Equal opportunity in public employment.
* **Art 17:** Abolition of Untouchability
  *→ Think: "Untouchability is a Threat (17)."*

### **Right to Freedom (Art 19–22):**

* **Art 19 (Six Freedoms):** **S-A-A-M-R-P** — **S**peech, **A**ssembly, **A**ssociation, **M**ovement, **R**esidence, **P**rofession
  *→ Think: "SAAM has RP (Reputation Points)."*
* **Art 20:** Protection in convictions:

  * No **E**x-post-facto law
  * No **D**ouble jeopardy
  * No **S**elf-incrimination
    *→ Think: "Conviction protection: EDS (like Edit/End your suffering)."*
* **Art 21:** Life & Personal Liberty — Very wide: includes dignity, privacy, environment
  *→ Think: "21 = Legal age for a full life."*
* **Art 21A:** Right to Education — Free & compulsory for children 6–14 yrs

### **Right Against Exploitation (Art 23–24):**

* **Art 23:** Prohibits human trafficking & forced labour (begar)
* **Art 24:** No child labour (under 14) in hazardous work
  *→ Think: "2-4 (Two for) children's safety."*

### **Right to Constitutional Remedies (Art 32):**

* Called **"Heart and Soul"** of Constitution (Ambedkar)
* Right to approach SC for enforcement of FRs

#### **Writs: "He May Produce Certified Quotes"**

1. **H**abeas Corpus – unlawful detention
2. **M**andamus – order public authority to perform duty
3. **P**rohibition – stop lower court from exceeding jurisdiction
4. **C**ertiorari – quash lower court’s decision
5. **Q**uo Warranto – challenge authority of public office holder

## **IV. Directive Principles of State Policy (DPSP) (Part IV: Articles 36–51)**

* **Nature:** Moral guidance for socio-economic justice.
* **Non-Justiciable (Art 37):** Cannot be enforced, but "fundamental in governance."
* **Examples:** Equal pay (Art 39), UCC (Art 44), Panchayats (Art 40), Environment (Art 48A)
* **FRs vs. DPSP:**
  FRs = Negative, enforceable, political democracy
  DPSP = Positive, non-enforceable, socio-economic democracy
  *→ Think: FR = "My Rights NOW!" | DPSP = "Govt's To-Do List"*

---

## **V. Fundamental Duties (FDs) (Part IVA: Art 51A)**

* **Nature:** Moral duties for citizens
* **Added by:** 42nd Amendment (Swaran Singh Committee)
* **Examples:** Respect Constitution, cherish freedom struggle, promote harmony, protect environment
  *→ Think: "Citizen’s Code of Conduct."*

---

## **VI. The Union (Part V)**

### **President (Art 52–62, 72):**

* **Nominal Head**
* **Impeachment (Art 61):** For constitutional violation
* **Pardoning Power (Art 72):** Pardon, reprieve, respite, remit, commute
  *→ Think: "President's 7-2 (Saat Khoon Maaf) powers"*

### **PM & Council of Ministers (Art 74, 75):**

* **Real Executive**
* **Art 74:** Advice of CoM is binding
* **Art 75:** PM appointed by President; CoM collectively responsible to LS
  *→ Think: "CoM swims or sinks together in LS."*

### **Attorney General (Art 76):**

* Highest Law Officer. Can speak in Parliament (no vote)
  *→ Think: "Govt’s Lawyer No. 1."*

### **Parliament (Art 79–122):**

* **LS (People)** vs **RS (States)**
* **Money Bill (Art 110):** Only in LS; RS has 14-day suggestion window
* **Joint Sitting (Art 108):** For Ordinary Bills (not Money Bills); presided by LS Speaker

---

## **VII. The States (Part VI)**

* **Governor (Art 153):** Nominal head; agent of Centre

  * **Pardoning Power (Art 161):** For state laws
* **CM & CoM (Art 163, 164):** Real executive at state level
* **Legislature:**

  * **VS (Vidhan Sabha):** Like LS
  * **VP (Vidhan Parishad):** Like RS (not in all states)

---

## **VIII. The Judiciary**

### **Supreme Court (Art 124–147):**

* Apex court, guardian of Constitution
* **Jurisdiction:**

  * **Original:** Centre-State disputes
  * **Appellate:** Civil/criminal appeals
  * **Advisory (Art 143):** Presidential reference
  * **Writs (Art 32):** FR enforcement

### **High Courts (Art 214–231):**

* Highest court at state level
* **Art 226:** Broader writ powers (FRs + other rights)

---

## **IX. Union–State Relations (Part XI & XII)**

### **Legislative (Art 245–255):**

* **7th Schedule Lists:**

  * **List I (Union):** Defence, Currency
  * **List II (State):** Police, Health
  * **List III (Concurrent):** Education, Forests
* **Residuary Powers (Art 248):** With Parliament
* **Art 254:** Union law prevails in case of conflict in Concurrent list

### **Financial Relations:**

* **Finance Commission (Art 280):** Recommends tax distribution

---

## **X. Emergency Provisions (Part XVIII)**

### **National Emergency (Art 352):**

* Grounds: **WAR** – **W**ar, **A**rmed Rebellion, **E**xternal Aggression
* Effects: FRs (esp. Art 19) suspended; Centre gets overriding power

### **State Emergency (Art 356):**

* Failure of constitutional machinery in state

### **Financial Emergency (Art 360):**

* Threat to financial stability; never invoked

---

## **XI. Amendment of the Constitution (Part XX)**

* **Art 368:** Parliament's power to amend
* **Procedures:**

  1. Simple Majority
  2. Special Majority
  3. Special Majority + State Ratification (federal matters)
* **Basic Structure Doctrine:** Cannot be destroyed by amendment

---

## **XII. Constitutional Bodies & Misc.**

* **CAG (Art 148):** Guardian of public funds; reports to President/Governor
* **Union Territories:** Run by President via Administrator (e.g., Lt. Gov.)
* **Administrative Tribunals (Art 323A/B):** Handle service matters
* **UPSC/SPSC (Art 315):** Conduct recruitment exams
* **Art 301:** Freedom of trade, commerce, and intercourse throughout India
